## Judgment at a Glance
1. **Introduction**: This judgment pertains to SC/ST No.3/2021 in the SC/ST Court, R.R. District, addressing allegations under The Scheduled Caste and Scheduled Tribe (POA) Amendment Act, 2015 (Sections 3(1)(s) and 3(2)(va)) and Section 506 IPC. The accused, Danda Srinivas Rao (A2) and Smt. Danda Shiva Kumari (A3), both OC (Chowday), were accused of caste-based abuse and criminal intimidation against a complainant from the SC-Adi Andhra Mala community.

2. **Prosecution/Plaintiff's Case**: The prosecution alleged that the accused abused the complainant in public view using casteist slurs, following a prior stalking case filed by the daughter of the accused. Evidence included testimonies from the complainant, his father, and witnesses (e.g., PW.3, an elder and colony president) who corroborated the abuse. The prosecution emphasized the public nature of the incident and the accused's knowledge of the complainant's caste.

3. **Defense Strategy**: The defense challenged the independence of witnesses, particularly PW.3, citing their long-standing association with the complainant's family. They highlighted typographical errors in the FIR, lack of independent witnesses, and the complainant's criminal antecedents (e.g., prior stalking case) to suggest bias and weaken the prosecution's case.

4. **The Ratio Decidendi**: The court found the accused guilty under Section 3(1)(s) of the SC/ST (POA) Amendment Act, 2015, which criminalizes caste-based abuse in public view. The court held that the presence of witnesses (even with alleged associations) and the public nature of the incident satisfied statutory requirements. However, the accused were acquitted under Section 3(2)(va) (criminal intimidation) and Section 506 IPC due to insufficient evidence of causing alarm beyond reasonable doubt.

5. **Final Holding**: Accused No.2 & 3 were convicted under Section 3(1)(s) of the SC/ST (POA) Amendment Act, 2015, and sentenced to five years rigorous imprisonment and a fine of Rs.10,000 each. They were acquitted of charges under Section 3(2)(va) of the Act and Section 506 IPC. The judgment also set aside any remand period under Section 428 Cr.P.C. and informed the accused of their right to appeal.
