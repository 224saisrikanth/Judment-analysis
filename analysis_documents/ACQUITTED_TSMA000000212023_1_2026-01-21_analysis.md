## Judgment at a Glance
1. **Introduction**: This case involves a rape and sexual assault charge under sections 376(2)(n) of the IPC and section 5(1) r/w 6 of the POCSO Act, 2012, filed by the State of Telangana against the accused, identified as Rekhya Naik, a 27-year-old Lambada driver from Bandameedi Thanda, Keshavapur village. The case was heard by the Fast Track Special Sessions Judge for Expeditious Trial and Disposal of Rape and POCSO Act Cases in Mahabubnagar on 21 January 2026.

2. **Prosecution/Plaintiff's Case**: The prosecution alleged that the accused eloped with the victim, a 15-year-old girl studying in NTR Junior College, under the pretext of love and marriage. They claimed the accused sexually assaulted the victim multiple times after taking her to his rented house in Marlu, Sheshadrinagar. Evidence included the victim's and her mother's testimonies, police reports, medical examination reports, and forensic analysis, though the latter did not find conclusive evidence of sexual assault.

3. **Defense Strategy**: The defense argued that the victim and her mother turned hostile during the trial, providing no support for the prosecution's case. They highlighted the lack of physical evidence and the accused's denial of all allegations, emphasizing that the prosecution failed to prove guilt beyond reasonable doubt.

4. **The Ratio Decidendi**: The court held that the prosecution did not meet the burden of proof required to establish the accused's guilt beyond reasonable doubt. The hostile testimonies of the victim and her mother, coupled with insufficient corroborating evidence, led to the conclusion that the case against the accused was not substantiated.

5. **Final Holding**: The accused was acquitted under section 235(1) Cr.P.C. The bail bonds were to be canceled after the appeal period under section 437 Cr.P.C., and unmarked non-valuable case properties were to be destroyed post-appeal period.

This is a critical procedural lapse, as their testimony was central to the prosecution's case. #### Forensic/Medical Department:
- **Chain of Custody Concerns**: While the victim was examined medically and FSL reports were obtained, the court noted that the victim was not examined by the police, raising questions about the proper handling and documentation of forensic evidence. The FSL report did not find semen or blood, and the medical report was inconclusive, further weakening the forensic chain.
